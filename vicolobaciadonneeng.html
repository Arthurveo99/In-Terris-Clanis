Città della Pieve (PG)

A Warm Brick Embrace in the Ancient Heart of Città della Pieve

In the heart of the historic center of Città della Pieve, nestled among the red-brick houses that color the narrow Umbrian streets, lies a corner where history, architecture, and legend meet: the Vicolo Baciadonne. At just 53 centimeters wide at its narrowest point—and rarely more than 80—it is considered one of the narrowest alleys in Italy, if not in all of Europe. But it’s not just the measurements that make it special: it’s the name, the origin, and the unique atmosphere that still lingers today.

A story carved in brick

The origins of this narrow passage date back to the Middle Ages, when the town was divided into terzieri (districts) and rivalries between neighboring families were a daily occurrence. Legend has it that the alley was born out of a dispute between two families who, despite living side by side, wanted to keep as much distance as possible from one another. They left just enough space for a person to pass through—unintentionally creating a tiny urban masterpiece.

But the people, as they often do, turned a sign of division into a symbol of unity: Vicolo Baciadonne (literally “Kiss-the-Women Alley”) earned its name because two people passing through it can’t help but brush against each other… and perhaps even steal a kiss.

A sensory experience

Walking through Vicolo Baciadonne is more than just a tourist gesture—it’s an experience for the senses. The ancient brick walls, smoothed by time and moisture, seem to close in around the visitor, encouraging a slower pace, inviting one to look up, to breathe in the scent of stone and history. Each step is a journey into the past, an immersion in the daily life of a town that has preserved its authentic soul.

And at the end of the alley, as a reward for the attentive observer, a breathtaking view opens up over the Val di Chiana and the distant silhouette of Mount Cetona, silent witnesses to centuries of history and landscape.

A symbol of romance

Today, Vicolo Baciadonne is one of the most photographed spots in Città della Pieve. During Valentine’s Day, it is often decorated with hearts, flowers, and soft lights, becoming a true “street of love.” It hosts romantic events, flash mobs, and even marriage proposals, as couples choose this unique setting to seal their eternal bond.

A microcosm of Umbrian beauty

In its silence and simplicity, Vicolo Baciadonne encapsulates the very essence of Città della Pieve: a masterful blend of medieval architectural rigor and heartfelt warmth, of stone and poetry. It’s a place you can walk through in seconds—but it stays with you forever.
